# Sulphur Springs 2025 - An escalation of quantity and quality

The 2025 Canadian ultramarathon season was bigger than ever, with 200+ races and over 15,000 finishers. But amidst this explosive growth, the **Sulphur Springs Trail Run** has managed something rare: it hasn't just grown in size—it has fundamentally evolved in quality.

The Sulphur Springs trail system in Ancaster has housed this event for [33 years](https://sulphursprings.burlingtonrunners.com/), making it the oldest running ultramarathon in Canada. The race features a 20K loop course offering a runner's paradise for all skill levels. It's mainly groomed trails with some technical elements, and a not-insignificant amount of hills. Each loop of the Sulphur Springs 20km course has 500m of elevation gain.

From shattering decades-old course records to serving as an essential gateway to the legendary Western States 100, here is why my local ultra is still one of the most significant events in Canada.

***

Trail and ultra running had steady growth for over a decade, and Sulphur Springs is certainly a large part of that. Taking all the [Canadian Ultra results listed on DUV ](https://statistik.d-u-v.org/geteventlist.php?year=2025\&dist=all\&country=CAN\&Submit.x=18\&Submit.y=5\&label=\&surface=all\&sort=1\&from=\&to=)(ultramarathon results platform), Sulphur Springs has grown alongside Canadian ultras.

This analysis focuses exclusively on these ultra distances due to data accessibility, meaning the ranking doesn't even account for the high participation in the sub-ultra events, which saw an additional 355 finishers in the 20k and 255 in the 10k.

The Sulphur Springs men's winners established themselves as national leaders in 2025:

* **Paul Vanoostveen** ran a blistering **14:38:23** in the 100-Miler, establishing himself as one of the elite long-distance performers in Canada, hinting towards to his eventual [Burrito League dominance](https://runningmagazine.ca/the-scene/toronto-man-runs-nearly-1000-kilometres-for-free-burritos-and-cold-baths/).
* **Robert Brouillette** secured the 100k win in **8:07:22**, a time that ranked 2nd on the national leaderboard for 2025, and helped land him on Team Canada for the World 50km Championships in 2026.
* **Alex Forte** clocked a **3:23:02** 50k, placing him among the Top 5 fastest 50k runners in the country.

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/national_benchmarks_men.png)

### National Ranking: Women

The women's field at Sulphur was equally deep, producing national-caliber performances across all distances:

* **Molly Hurford** dominated the 100-Miler in **17:22:53**, placing her as a top contender on the national 100-mile scene.
* **Karen Holland** won the 100k in **9:55:14**, showcasing world-class endurance and consistency.

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/national_benchmarks_women.png "Women's Top 10 Times in Canada")

All of these amazing times were done on a course few would choose to run a fast time. It's all gravel trail, some technical elements, and a significant amount of elevation gain. Each loop of the Sulphur Springs 20km course has 500m of elevation gain. To try to accurately reflect that let's recalculate these times by normalizing for elevation gain with Grade Adjusted Pace.

### Grade Adjusted Pace (GAP)

Comparing Grade Adjusted Pace (GAP) to actual pace reveals how the terrain uniquely impacts the field, and how the top performers manage their effort across the climbs and descents. GAP is an estimated pace that adjusts for elevation changes, calculated using a physics-based model that accounts for the extra energy required to run uphill and the reduced energy (up to a point) when running downhill. This allows us to compare the true effort required on a hilly course like Sulphur Springs to running on a flat road.

## 3. Masterclass in Consistency: How the Winners Won

It’s one thing to be fast; it’s another to be efficient. Our sub-lap analysis reveals that the 2025 champions didn't just run harder—they ran smarter.

### Pacing Strategy

Look at the pacing profile for **Alex Forte (50k winner)**. While the field often sees a sharp "degradation" (slowing down) in later laps, the winners maintained a near-flat pace throughout.

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/50k_2025_profile.png)

### The Carnage Factor

Our **"Carnage Charts"** (an idea adapted from [John Sugden's substack Over the Ridgeline](https://johnsug.substack.com/)) show how effectively the top runners navigated the field. While others succumbed to the undulating terrain, the winners steadily climbed through the rankings or maintained an iron grip on the lead with metronomic consistency.

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/50k_2025_carnage.png)

### The Mathematics of Slowing Down

To quantify this consistency, we ran a linear slope analysis on every runner's lap paces from 2022 to 2025 to see exactly what percentage they "slowed down" from their first lap to their last.

The results highlight a massive disparity between the winners and the rest of the field:

* **50k Winners** are pacing masters, only slowing down by **1% to 3%** over the entire race (compared to a **22% to 37%** slowdown for the field average).
* **100M Winners** slow down by roughly **37% to 55%**, but the rest of the 100-mile field degrades by a brutal **65% to 77%**.
* We even found a rare **negative split** in our dataset—Mike Fickel managed a -7% pace acceleration in his 2022 100k victory.

## 5. The Western States Gateway

Perhaps the most significant shift in recent years was Sulphur Springs becoming an official **Western States 100 Qualifier** in 2024. The impact on participation in the "long" distances (100k and 100M) was immediate and massive.

### The Qualifier Surge

Since joining the WS circuit, the number of finishers aiming for a qualifier spot has skyrocketed, turning the event into a high-stakes arena for runners across the continent.

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/ws_qualifying_100k.png)![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/ws_qualifying_100m.png)
![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/ss_long_distance_surge.png)

![](/uploads/SulphurSprings/participation_stacked.png)explain how the 100k and 100M used to make up X% of the entrants, but now is Y%.

### A Massive Catchment Area

Why such growth? Because Sulphur Springs serves as the primary qualifier for a massive population center. Our catchment analysis shows that for millions of people in Ontario, Quebec, and the Northeast US, Sulphur is the closest and most efficient path to Squaw Valley.

This analysis was done by retrieving the exact coordinates for the largest population centers in Canada and the US, computing the closest geographical spherical distance ("as the crow flies") using the haversine formula to any available Western States qualifying race, and generating a Voronoi diagram based on those closest-race designations.

## 6. International Prestige: The UTMB Index

While Sulphur Springs dominates nationally, how does it stack up internationally? Using the UTMB Index, we calculated the **Race Competitiveness Index (RCI)** for Sulphur Springs over the years. This metric, pioneered by Travis Loncar at [Freetrail](https://freetrail.com/), calculates the field structure by taking the average UTMB Index score from the top finishers.

The results show a clear upward trajectory. The depth of competition is increasing, meaning it requires a higher and higher UTMB Index simply to finish in the top 10.
