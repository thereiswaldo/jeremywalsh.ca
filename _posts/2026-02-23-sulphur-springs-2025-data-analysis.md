# Why Sulphur Springs 2025 Set a New Bar for Canadian Ultras

The 2025 Canadian ultramarathon season was bigger than ever, with 200+ races and over 15,000 finishers. But amidst this explosive growth, the **Sulphur Springs Trail Run** has managed something rare: it hasn't just grown in size—it has fundamentally evolved in quality.

From shattering decades-old course records to serving as an essential gateway to the legendary Western States 100, here is why Sulphur Springs is one of the most significant events in Canada.

***

## 1. The Performance Surge: Competitive Beyond the Region

For years, Sulphur Springs was seen as a regional staple. Today, the data tells a different story. By analyzing the **UTMB Index** (a global measure of runner performance), we can see a clear upward trend in competitiveness.

The "Modern Era" (2017 onwards) has seen a staggering progression at Sulphur Springs. In 2025 alone, the event produced some of the fastest ultra times in the country for both Men and Women.

### National Ranking: Men

The Sulphur Springs men's winners established themselves as national leaders in 2025:

* **Paul Vanoostveen** ran a blistering **14:38:23** in the 100-Miler, establishing himself as one of the elite long-distance performers in Canada.
* **Robert Brouillette** secured the 100k win in **8:07:22**, a time that ranks near the top of the national leaderboard.
* **Juan Zorrilla** won the 50-Mile race in **7:36:46**, placing prominently in the national Top 10.
* **Alex Forte** clocked a **3:23:02** 50k, placing him among the Top 5 fastest 50k runners in the country.

![](/uploads/national_benchmarks_men.png)

### National Ranking: Women

The women's field at Sulphur was equally deep, producing national-caliber performances across all distances:

* **Molly Hurford** dominated the 100-Miler in **17:22:53**, placing her as a top contender on the national 100-mile scene.
* **Karen Holland** won the 100k in **9:55:14**, showcasing world-class endurance and consistency.
* **Rina Atienza** took the 50-Mile crown in **9:00:58**, securing a spot in the national rankings.
* **Tanis Bolton** secured the 50k victory in **4:14:07**, establishing a high bar for excellence in the distance.

## 2. A Decade of Dominance: The "Sulphur Effect"

Sulphur Springs doesn't just produce fast times occasionally—it has consistently dominated the national leaderboard for over 15 years.

Our **"Top 10 Density"** analysis tracks how many of the fastest 10 times in Canada each year come from Sulphur Springs. In the premier long distances (100k and 100M), Sulphur frequently accounts for **5 to 9 of the top 10 national times** annually. ## 4. The Western States Gateway

Perhaps the most significant shift in recent years was Sulphur Springs becoming an official **Western States 100 Qualifier** in 2024. The impact on participation in the "long" distances (100k and 100M) was immediate and massive.

### The Qualifier Surge

Since joining the WS circuit, the number of finishers aiming for a qualifier spot has skyrocketed, turning the event into a high-stakes arena for runners across the continent.

![](/uploads/ws_qualifying_100k.png)![](/uploads/ws_qualifying_100m.png)

### A Massive Catchment Area

Why such growth? Because Sulphur Springs serves as the primary qualifier for a massive population center. Our catchment analysis shows that for millions of people in Ontario, Quebec, and the Northeast US, Sulphur is the closest and most efficient path to Squaw Valley.
